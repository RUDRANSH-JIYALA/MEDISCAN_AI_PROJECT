About MediScan AI

Deep learning segmentation for Brain, Cardiac, and Prostate MRI.

AI-Powered Medical Imaging Platform

MediScan AI uses state-of-the-art DeepLabV3+ with ResNet-50 backbone to perform pixel-level segmentation on MRI scans across three organ types. The primary model achieves Dice scores above 0.99 on held-out test sets, demonstrating strong generalisation across diverse patient cohorts.

🔬

Segmentation Pipeline

DeepLabV3+ with ASPP (atrous rates 6, 12, 18) captures multi-scale context. ResNet-50 backbone provides deep hierarchical features. Combined BCE + Dice loss for class-balanced training.

🛡️

Privacy First

All processing occurs client-side in your browser. No MRI images or patient data are ever uploaded to external servers. Your data never leaves your device.

📊

Clinical Metrics

Dice Coefficient, IoU (Jaccard Index), and confidence scores are computed for each analysis. Risk classification thresholds match organ-specific clinical literature guidelines.

Model Architecture

🟣

DeepLabV3+ (Head)

Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling (ASPP) with parallel dilated convolutions at rates 6, 12, 18 captures context at multiple scales. Encoder–decoder with 3×3 convolutions and bilinear upsampling for precise boundary localisation.

ASPP Multi-scale BCE+Dice Loss Threshold 0.5
Brain: 0.9914 Cardiac: 0.9903 Prostate: 0.9935
🔷

ResNet-50 (Encoder)

50-layer residual network providing hierarchical feature extraction. Residual (skip) connections prevent vanishing gradients in deep networks. Shallow layers learn low-level MRI textures; deep layers learn semantic structures.

Residual Blocks 50 Layers Skip Connections
conv1 → pool layer1–4 64→512ch
🔷

U-Net (Comparison)

Encoder–decoder CNN with symmetric skip connections. The encoder contracts (64→512 channels, MaxPool) while the decoder expands via bilinear upsampling. Skip connections concatenate encoder feature maps into the decoder, preserving fine edge detail critical for organ boundary segmentation.

Skip Connections BCE+Dice Loss Fine Boundaries Bottleneck 1024ch
Encoder 64→512ch 4 Downsampling Blocks Sigmoid Output
🔹

SegNet (Comparison)

VGG-16–based encoder saves MaxPool indices during downsampling. The symmetric decoder uses these stored indices for unpooling (no skip connections) — reconstructing spatial resolution without needing to store full encoder feature maps. Memory-efficient and fast at inference time.

Pooling Indices VGG-16 Encoder Memory-Efficient Fast Inference
5 Pooling Stages Index-Guided Upsample No Skip Connections

How Each Organ Inference Works

🧠

Brain MRI

  • Input: PNG/JPG or NIfTI (.nii.gz), resized to 128×128 grayscale
  • NIfTI handling: Loads FLAIR channel, extracts middle axial slice (z//2)
  • Normalisation: Zero-mean unit-std per slice
  • Model: DeepLabV3+ Baseline (also available: U-Net, SegNet)
  • Output: Sigmoid probability map → threshold 0.5 → binary mask
  • Risk: Area >8% → High; 3–8% → Medium; <3% → Low
❤️

Cardiac MRI

  • Input: PNG/JPG, resized to 256×256 grayscale, normalised [0,1]
  • Loss: BCE + Dice (custom bce_dice_loss)
  • Model: DeepLabV3+ + ResNet-50 (also: U-Net, SegNet)
  • Output: Sigmoid → threshold 0.5 → area% + volume_cc
  • Risk: Area >35% → Cardiomegaly; 20–35% → Mild; <20% → Normal
  • Confidence: prob_map.mean() × 100
🔵

Prostate MRI

  • Input: mpMRI PNG/JPG, 256×256 grayscale, normalised [0,1]
  • Metric: Dice Coefficient with smoothing (smooth=1)
  • Model: DeepLabV3+ + ResNet-50 (Dice: 0.9935)
  • Output: Sigmoid → threshold 0.5 → volume_cc estimation
  • Risk: Vol >1.2cc → High; 0.8–1.2cc → Moderate; <0.8cc → Normal
  • Confidence: mean(|prob_map−0.5|)×200, capped at 99.9%
